About Hampta Pass Trek:
Hampta Pass is perched at an elevation of 4,270m (14039ft) on the Pir Panjal range in the Himalayas. It is a small passage between the Lahaul Chandra Valley and Kullu Valley in Himachal Pradesh. Hampta Pass Trek is one of the best and convenient treks in Manali, Himachal Pradesh.
Hampta Pass adventure trek will take you to the valley of wildflowers, alpine Meadows of conifer forest, rock walls, amazing waterfalls, snow-white glaciers, and a lot more. The trail takes you to the lush green valleys of Kullu. Hampta Pass serves as a balcony view to a different new world while crossing the pass you see the Lahaul Valley deserts all surrounded by mountains.
Take the Hampta Pass Trek for a splendid route that will lead you to the heart of the Himalayas. One of the most beautiful and amazing moderate level trek in Himachal, the Hampta Pass Trek takes you through varied landscapes from green meadows, pristine rivers to glacial valleys and imposing Himalayan ranges. This guided trek along the Hampta Pass circuit is ideal for budget travellers looking for a cost-effective trek in the Himalayas!
It offers you the view of snow-capped Himalayan peaks, the translucent Chandratal lake, along the rivulets of River Ravi through lush meadows and forests. The 5-day Hampta Pass trek takes you through an enchanting and unadulterated beauty of the Pir Panjal ranges of the Himalayas. The Hampta Pass Trek difficulty level is moderate.


Things to look forward to in Hampta Pass:
1. Drive from Manali to Jobra
To enjoy Manali, a drive from Manali to Jobra is one of the best options. Manali to Jobra is the most excellent mountain drive in the country. You will be left glued to the window as you will witness the sudden change of landscape and forests. You can see many things from apple orchards to deodar forests to oaks. For a better view of the natural beauty, you need to sit on the left side of the vehicle. As you move forward, it becomes cooler and cooler.

2. The Forest section
The forest is small and comes on the first day of the Hampta pass trek. This small forest section is a rare one on the trek and will leave you mesmerised. During this particular section, you can locate oaks, birch, and maple trees together and stays in my memory

3. The exciting Pass Crossing
Pass crossings are usually very exciting. The pass crossing in the Hampta Pass crossing is more adventure and dramatic. The pass crossing is like walking through a gorge. The valley is really narrow, with stark brown walls on either side and deep white snow below you. Your voices will echo, and on both sides, the ice columns dripped down on some caves.
You get to the pass by climbing three ledges one after the other. The trails leading to the pass is truly an adventure. Unfortunately, you will not be able to see the other side immediately as you reach the pass. The pass is a long, deep way with bending to the left and drops off in an opening high up in the mountains.

4.Kullu and Lahaul valleys
One of the big highlights of doing the Hampta pass trek is the Lahaul valley. The valley will make you stunned. You can see the entire Shea Goru valley many thousand feet below the Lahaul valley's first sight. You can not even imagine the visual you see from the top. The Kullu valley will be behind you with its dense trees, grasslands , tall cliffs and flowers. And in the front, you can see Lahaul valley. The place is a different greenish desert.

5. The campsites of Balu Ka Ghera and Shea Goru
Choose the campsites of Shea Goru and Balu Ka Gera on the Hampta pass trek. These camps are excellent with good facilities. Shea Goru on the Lahaul side is the most preferred campsite by the trekkers because of its green surroundings. The camp also has a stream in front and is surrounded by mountains. The Balu Ka Ghera is also fantastic and is located on the edges of the river delta. The Hampta Pass is emerging right in front of the campsite. The campsite has all facilities and amazing surroundings.

6. Descent to Lahaul
Getting down the Hampta pass is more thrilling than climbing the Hampta Pass. In the descent of six hours, you can experience a massive change in landscape several times. Initially, you can witness the huge snow-capped mountains on both sides, then the slides and plunges. Finally, you can also witness the grassy trail facing the Chandrabhaga river. You can relax here for a long time.

Hampta Pass Trek Safety ‚Äì Weather
As mentioned earlier, the Hampta pass trek is ideal from June to September. However, trekking during these four months is entirely different. Therefore, you have to be careful a few times.

If you opt for June, the trail is slippery. There is a lot of snow on the trail, even from Balu Ka Ghera. So it is recommended to use the right gear like microspikes, gaiters, etc. If you opt for July and August, rain is a common occurrence as these months are the monsoon season. During these months, the trail remains slippery and tricky. Also, the streams will flow strongly, and you have to be extra careful while crossing the river.

Hampta Pass Trek - Exit points The Hampta Pass trek has only limited exits as this trek is a pass crossing trek. Suppose you face any issues before crossing the pass, head down to Jobra before moving on to Manali. If you face any issues after crossing the pass, you will have to move to Chatru.
